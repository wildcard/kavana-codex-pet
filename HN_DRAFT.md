## First comment

Hi HN — we made Kavana, a black-and-tan Shiba Inu companion for Codex Desktop.

She started as Karo, a real and much-loved dog, then became a custom Codex pet through the new pet tooling. The package includes all nine standard Codex animation states, sixteen pointer-aware look directions, source frames, row strips, previews, deterministic checksums, cross-platform install helpers, and mobile-sync repair tooling.

The website is also a live playground for the same atlas: you can give her the zoomies, send her roaming around a little trail, ask her to wave, jump, look in all sixteen directions, work, review, or take a nap. The top sequence loops through those behaviors automatically, while reduced-motion mode keeps each one available as a persistent still pose.

Kavana also became a community contribution to Project Caro, an open-source terminal safety agent. On Caro’s site she roams around, chats about the project, can be dragged to a favorite spot, falls asleep, remembers whether you tucked her away, and actually trots offscreen when dismissed.

The repository is intentionally transparent about provenance and privacy: the curated derived pet assets are public, while raw private photos, references, prompts, logs, and local Codex state are excluded.

We were inspired by mySebbe’s Malou Codex Pet repository and tried to give Kavana the same complete public-project treatment while preserving the newer v2 look-direction support.

Happy Codexing — feedback on installation, animation readability, and the broader pet feature is very welcome. ❤️
